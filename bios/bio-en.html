Patrick Billy – Official Biography

Patrick Billy is a French designer of digital accessibility tools, inclusive interfaces, and applied AI solutions for real-world environments. His work bridges technology and human reality: disability, constrained environments, low-tech contexts and complex social situations.

Coming from a background in graphic design, training and field-oriented problem solving, Patrick has learned how to build solutions that are simple, robust and accessible. He is the founder of Élan pour Tous, a social initiative that creates tools aimed at supporting vulnerable users, families, teachers and organizations.

He is also the creator of Mini-WTD (Work-Test-Demo), a lightweight simulator used to analyse job tasks, assess workplace constraints and propose concrete accommodations. Mini-WTD is intentionally designed to work in environments with limited digital infrastructure.

Patrick works between France and Madagascar, with a strong sensitivity for contexts where digital tools must adapt to human reality, not the other way around. His approach combines:

  • Digital accessibility (RGAA / WCAG)
  • Inclusive & low-tech UX design
  • Applied AI for explanation, guidance and simplification
  • Offline and constrained-environment tools (USB, intranet)
  • Field analysis, job simulation and workplace adaptation

His work is guided by a clear philosophy: technology must reduce complexity, not create it.
